Executive Summary Direkta is a software platform that enables German property owners to sell their real estate themselves — faster, more transparently, and at a lower cost than working with a traditional real estate agent (Makler). The product is not an AI tool wrapped around listing creation. It is a complete, guided sales process: from preparing the listing, to pricing the property correctly, to filtering and qualifying buyers, to evaluating offers and handing the seller into the notarisation process. This document defines what must be built (Lastenheft — functional requirements from the user's perspective) and how it must be built (Pflichtenheft — technical specification from the engineering perspective). It is the binding scope reference for the MVP and the source of truth for engineering, product, and founders during the first eight weeks of development. 1.1 Why this exists In Germany, selling a property privately is technically legal and financially attractive — a typical Maklerprovision of 3.57% (incl. VAT) on a €500,000 property is roughly €17,850 paid by the seller. Yet over 70% of all residential transactions still go through agents. The reason is not value; it is process complexity. Owners do not know how to price, how to write a compliant listing, how to filter serious buyers from tourists, or how to safely move a deal toward the notary. Direkta closes that gap with software. 1.2 The single MVP hypothesis &quot;Property owners can use Direkta to sell their property themselves — faster or cheaper than with a traditional agent — without needing to understand the real estate process.&quot; Everything in this specification serves this single hypothesis. Anything that does not measurably contribute to time-to-sale, achieved sale price, or cost saved versus an agent, is out of scope for the MVP. 1.3 The four core modules The MVP consists of four product modules plus two agentic modules that operate across the others. Anything outside these is explicitly out of scope (see Section 14). # Module Purpose M1 Smart Listing Creation Generate a compliant, high-quality property listing from photos and structured input within 15 minutes. M2 Pricing Engine Produce a defensible price recommendation with three explicit pricing strategies, based on comparable transactions and property attributes. M3 Lead Funnel &amp; Qualification Capture buyer enquiries, qualify them automatically (budget, financing, timing), and let the seller schedule viewings without manual coordination. M4 Offer Dashboard Receive, score, and rank offers; provide a clear recommendation; hand the seller into the notarisation workflow. M5 Exposé Agent Conversational agent that creates the complete Exposé in dialog with the seller — replacing form-based entry for sellers who prefer guided onboarding. M6 Listing Agent Bidirectional syndication agent: pushes the listing to ImmobilienScout24, pulls back views, contacts, bookmarks, and ingests new leads. 1.4 Success criteria The MVP is considered successful when, across at least 25 completed transactions: Median time from listing to signed Notarvertrag is below the regional Makler benchmark for comparable property types. Net proceeds to the seller (after Direkta fee) are equal to or higher than the equivalent broker-mediated sale. Net Promoter Score from sellers is ≥ 50. Less than 5% of sales require post-hoc human intervention by Direkta staff. 2. Market Positioning &amp; Vision 2.1 The market The German residential real estate market sees roughly 800,000 to 1,000,000 ownership transactions per year across all property types, with a total transaction volume in the order of €200–250 billion. The dominant intermediary is the Makler (real estate agent), historically charging 5.95–7.14% in total commission split between buyer and seller. Since the Bestellerprinzip reform of December 2020, commissions on residential sales must be split roughly equally between buyer and seller, but the absolute amounts have not declined. Two structural shifts make a software-first alternative viable now: Sellers increasingly start their journey on portals (ImmobilienScout24, Immowelt, Kleinanzeigen) and only later engage an agent. The first 30 minutes of seller intent are now digital. LLM-grade text generation, image enhancement and structured-data reasoning have collapsed the cost of producing a professional-looking listing from hundreds of euros (or hours of agent time) to single-digit cents per listing. 2.2 Competitive landscape Direkta competes against four distinct categories of incumbents. Understanding which one we are taking share from in any given conversation is critical to product, pricing and messaging decisions. Category Examples Their offer Where Direkta wins Traditional full-service Makler Engel &amp; Völkers, Von Poll, regional Makler Full-service, in-person, high-touch, 3.57% commission seller-side Cost: 90% cheaper. Transparency: seller sees every lead and offer. Hybrid online brokers McMakler, Homeday Reduced fee, fixed price option, central pool of agents True end-to-end self-serve. No call centre. Faster onboarding. Listing portals ImmobilienScout24, Immowelt, Kleinanzeigen Distribution only — seller still has to write listing, price, qualify, negotiate, contract Direkta does the work of an agent on top of the listing. DIY private sale PDF-Inserate, Word-Exposé, Excel-Bewertung Free but high effort, low quality, slow Comparable cost (or modest fee), drastically higher quality and speed. 2.3 Positioning statement &quot;Direkta is for property owners who want to sell their home without surrendering 3.57% to an agent — but who also want a guided, professional process they can trust. We are not a listing portal. We are not a marketplace. We are a software-driven sales process that replaces the agent.&quot; 2.4 What Direkta is not This is as important as what Direkta is. The product is intentionally not: A property portal. We do not aggregate listings for buyers. Buyers find Direkta listings through portals, search and direct landing pages. An iBuyer. We do not buy property from sellers. We help them sell to a third party. A property valuation tool. Valuation is a feature, not the product. An AI writing assistant. AI is plumbing. The product is the sales process. A marketplace with two-sided liquidity. Each listing has one seller and N buyers. We never optimise for buyer retention at the cost of seller outcomes. 2.5 Long-term vision (post-MVP) Beyond the MVP, Direkta accumulates a structural moat that no portal and no Makler can replicate: Transaction data: every closed sale produces a verified data point of (property attributes → final price → time-to-sale). This is more accurate than any portal's listing data, which only knows asking prices. Buyer graph: Direkta sees which buyers actually close, at what price, with what financing, and how long it took them. This is a unique buyer-quality signal. Pricing intelligence: with sufficient transaction volume, Direkta's price recommendation outperforms agent intuition in any given micro-market. Adjacent products: notary integration, financing brokerage, post-sale services, energy certificates. These are explicitly out of scope for the MVP. They are listed only to inform architectural decisions: the data model must be designed so that this future is not blocked. 3. Customer Perspective &amp; ICP 3.1 Primary customer: the seller Direkta has only one paying customer: the property owner who wants to sell. Buyers are users of the platform but not customers. This distinction must be enforced everywhere — in pricing, in support, in conflict resolution, and in product trade-offs. 3.1.1 Seller archetypes The MVP supports four seller archetypes. The product must serve all four, but conversion and onboarding flows are optimised primarily for archetypes A and B. Archetype Profile Trigger to sell Why they choose Direkta A — Estate seller Heir, 45–65, sells inherited property, often not in their city of residence Inheritance, tax deadline (10-year speculation period), Erbengemeinschaft pressure Low effort, geographic independence, transparent process, no agent commission on already-emotional asset B — Upgrader Family, 35–50, sells current home to buy bigger one Family growth, second child, remote work permanence Speed, control over timing to align with the new purchase, retain commission for down payment C — Downsizer Empty-nester or retiree, 55–75 Children moved out, partner's death, mobility, capital release Trust through transparency. Wants to understand every step. Sensitive to risk. D — Investor seller Owner of one or more rental properties, 40–65 Portfolio rebalancing, tax optimisation, exit Cost-driven. Calculates commission savings against opportunity cost of own time. 3.1.2 Jobs to be done From the seller's perspective, the underlying jobs are: &quot;Help me figure out what my property is worth — and prove it to me.&quot; &quot;Help me create a listing that doesn't look amateurish, without me writing it.&quot; &quot;Filter out tyre-kickers so I only spend time on serious buyers.&quot; &quot;Tell me which offer to take and why.&quot; &quot;Get me to the notary without surprises.&quot; Every feature in this document maps to one or more of these jobs. Features that map to none must be removed. 3.1.3 Pain points and trust barriers Sellers do not buy software. They buy confidence. The dominant trust barriers are: &quot;What if I price it wrong and lose money?&quot; — addressed by transparent comparables and three explicit pricing strategies. &quot;What if I miss a legal requirement (Energieausweis, Baulasten, etc.) and get sued?&quot; — addressed by a compliance checklist that blocks publishing until satisfied. &quot;What if a buyer wastes my time?&quot; — addressed by automatic qualification before any human contact. &quot;What if I can't tell if an offer is real or a scam?&quot; — addressed by the offer dashboard with risk scoring and identity verification. &quot;What if something goes wrong and I'm alone?&quot; — addressed by an explicit, optional human-support escalation lane. 3.2 Secondary user: the buyer Buyers are not paying customers but their experience directly affects seller outcomes. A poor buyer experience produces fewer or worse offers. The product must therefore meet a quality bar for buyers without optimising for them at the seller's expense. Buyer requirements are: Frictionless enquiry: a buyer can express interest in under 60 seconds, without creating an account. Honest information: buyers receive accurate, GEG- and Energieausweis-compliant property data, not marketing fluff. Predictable response: the buyer receives an automated acknowledgement within 5 minutes and a qualified next step within 24 hours. Privacy: buyer enquiries are not sold, syndicated or used to target advertising. 3.3 ICP for go-to-market The product scope covers all residential property types, but the initial customer acquisition should focus where unit economics, conversion and data quality are best. Recommended ICP focus for the first 100 listings: Property type: single-family homes (EFH), condominium apartments (ETW), and small multi-family (up to 4 units). Price band: €200,000 – €1,200,000. Geography: top-30 German cities and their commuter belts (sufficient comparables, sufficient buyer demand). Seller profile: archetypes A and B (see 3.1.1). The product must not technically restrict outside this ICP. The constraint is on marketing spend, not on product capability. 4. Product Scope (MVP) This section enumerates the four core modules of the MVP. Each module is described from the seller's perspective (what it does) before the functional and technical detail in later sections. 4.1 Module M1 — Smart Listing Creation Goal: the seller arrives with photos and basic facts about the property, and leaves with a complete, legally compliant, professional-looking listing in under 15 minutes. Inputs Property address (mandatory, validated against an address API). Property type (ETW, EFH, MFH, DHH, RH, Grundstück). Living area (m²), plot area (m²) where applicable, year built, number of rooms, number of bathrooms, floor (for ETW). Condition self-assessment on a five-point scale (Erstbezug, gepflegt, renovierungsbedürftig, sanierungsbedürftig, Rohbau/Neubau). Energy data: Energieausweis (upload PDF or enter values manually), heating type, energy class, primary energy consumption. Photos (minimum 6, recommended 12, maximum 30). Optional: floor plan, recent renovations, special features (balcony, garage, garden, fireplace, etc.). Outputs A structured property record persisted in the database. AI-generated long-form description (German, 250–600 words) following established Exposé conventions. AI-generated short summary (max. 160 characters, used for portals and social). Image-enhanced photos (perspective correction, white balance, light normalisation) — never AI-fabricated content. Compliance check: GEG mandatory fields present, Energieausweis valid, etc. Publishing is blocked until green. 4.2 Module M2 — Pricing Engine Goal: provide the seller with a defensible price recommendation that they can stand behind in negotiation, including three explicit strategy variants. Inputs Structured property record from M1. Comparable transactions and listings within a configurable radius and time window. Local market trend data (€/m² evolution over the last 24 months for the postcode). Outputs A recommended price band (low, median, high). Three strategy presets: Quick Sale (price target ≤ 5th percentile of comparables), Realistic (price target at median, expected time-to-sale), Maximum (price target at 90th percentile, longer expected time-to-sale). A list of comparable transactions (anonymised) used to derive the recommendation, with similarity scores. A confidence indicator (high / medium / low) based on number and similarity of comparables. Out of scope for the MVP: machine-learning models trained on proprietary data. The MVP uses a transparent, rule-based pricing function. This is intentional. The seller must be able to understand why a number was suggested. Black-box models break the trust contract. 4.3 Module M3 — Lead Funnel &amp; Qualification Goal: capture buyer interest, automatically separate serious buyers from noise, and let the seller schedule viewings without becoming a coordinator. Capture surface A unique landing page per property at direkta.de/{slug} or {city}.direkta.de/{slug}. An enquiry form embedded on the landing page (name, email, phone optional, free-text message). Inbound forwarding from listing portals (the portals send an email to a unique address per listing; Direkta parses it). Qualification flow Each new enquiry triggers an automated, conversational follow-up to the buyer asking three structured questions: stated budget, financing status (cash, pre-approved, in process, none), and intended purchase timing. The buyer's answers are scored and produce a Lead Quality Score from 0 to 100. Outputs to seller A ranked list of leads with quality score, financing status, and timing. Recommended action per lead (&quot;Schedule viewing&quot;, &quot;Request more info&quot;, &quot;Decline politely&quot;). One-click viewing scheduling against the seller's calendar (CalDAV or iCal export). Templated, reviewable messages to the buyer for each action. 4.4 Module M4 — Offer Dashboard Goal: when offers come in, give the seller a clear, defensible view of which offer to accept and why, and hand them safely into the notary process. Inputs Offer amount. Buyer identity (verified via lightweight KYC: photo ID upload + address). Financing proof (Finanzierungsbestätigung from a bank, or proof of liquid funds). Buyer-side conditions and timeline. Outputs Per-offer view: amount, deviation from asking price, financing strength score, timeline fit, risk flags. Ranked recommendation across all open offers with explicit reasoning. Side-by-side comparison view for the top three offers. &quot;Accept offer&quot; action that triggers the notary handoff workflow (generation of Reservierungsvereinbarung, list of required documents for the notary, suggested next steps). 4.5 Module M5 — Exposé Agent Goal: produce a complete, publication-ready Exposé through a conversational dialog with the seller — not a single LLM call against a static form. The Exposé Agent owns the entire creation surface: it asks targeted questions, requests missing information, drafts iteratively, self-reviews, and only completes when the result meets a defined quality bar. The Exposé Agent replaces the static form-based generation described in Module M1 for sellers who choose the guided path. M1 remains available as a fast-track option for power users who want to fill out a form. Behavioural model The Exposé Agent operates as a stateful, multi-turn conversational agent. It is not a chatbot. It is a goal-driven actor with a fixed objective — produce a publishable Exposé — and a bounded toolset. It has a fixed goal: produce a complete, GEG-compliant Exposé and a price band, ready for the seller to review and publish. It maintains a structured working memory of what is known, what is missing, and what has been confirmed by the seller. It asks one question at a time, in natural German, and never overwhelms the seller with form fields. It can request photo uploads, request a document (Energieausweis), or trigger a comparable-prices lookup — all via tool calls, not by handing the seller off to another screen. It self-reviews each draft against a quality rubric and re-drafts if any criterion fails. It hands control back to the seller with a clear &quot;Review &amp; Publish&quot; payload. Inputs Initial seller utterance (free text or button-click &quot;Start with the assistant&quot;). Optional pre-existing Property record if the seller already created one in M1. Photos uploaded mid-conversation. Energieausweis PDF uploaded mid-conversation. Outputs Fully populated Property record (same schema as M1). Long description and short summary, both edited and approved by the seller during the dialog. Comprehensive Exposé PDF (designed layout, ready to share via WhatsApp/email). A complete Conversation transcript stored against the Listing for audit and post-hoc improvement. A confidence flag if the agent had to make assumptions the seller did not explicitly confirm. Quality rubric (self-review) Before completing, the agent verifies internally that the draft satisfies every item below. If any item fails, the agent either re-drafts or asks the seller for the missing information. Mandatory GEG fields are present and consistent with the uploaded Energieausweis. Long description is between 250 and 600 words, in three paragraphs (property, surroundings, opportunity). No claim is made that is not traceable to seller input or uploaded data (no hallucinated features). No marketing superlatives that would breach typical Wettbewerbsrecht thresholds (&quot;einmalig&quot;, &quot;konkurrenzlos&quot;). At least 6 photos uploaded; at least one labelled as exterior or floor plan if applicable. Asking price is within the recommended price band, or seller has explicitly overridden it. Why agent, not single-prompt A single-prompt approach forces the seller to fill out a form before generation. That is exactly what every other tool already does. The agentic dialog converts onboarding from a 30-minute data-entry chore into a conversation that mirrors what a Makler does in person — but without the fee, the wait time, or the geographic constraint. This is the differentiator the seller can feel. 4.6 Module M6 — Listing Agent (Portal Syndication) Goal: publish, update and synchronise a Direkta listing on ImmobilienScout24 (IS24) without the seller manually copying data into a second system, and bring engagement signals (views, contacts, bookmarks) back into Direkta as a single source of truth. Scope (MVP) Primary target: ImmobilienScout24. Operations: publish, update, pause/unpause, withdraw. Inbound sync: pull views per day, contact requests, bookmarks (&quot;Vormerkungen&quot;), and forward parsed enquiries into Direkta as Leads. Out of scope for MVP: Immowelt, Kleinanzeigen, regional portals (architected as additional drivers behind the same interface). Integration approach — pragmatic with explicit migration path The MVP uses headless-browser automation against IS24 with the seller's own IS24 account credentials. This choice is made deliberately and with full awareness of the trade-offs documented in Section 11.4. The architecture is built around a portal-agnostic interface (PortalDriver), so that the underlying transport can be replaced — by the official IS24 API or by an OpenImmo XML feed — within two weeks of contract signing, without product code changes. This is explicitly documented as a tactical MVP choice, not a long-term position. Production traffic must be capped, monitored, and ready to be cut over to a compliant transport on short notice. Inputs A published Direkta Listing. The seller's IS24 account credentials, stored encrypted (see 12.3). Seller's explicit consent to use credentials on their behalf, recorded with timestamp. Outputs A SyndicationJob record per Listing × Portal with status (QUEUED, RUNNING, SUCCESS, FAILED, RETRYING). The IS24 listing ID once published, persisted on the SyndicationTarget. Daily PortalStat snapshots: impressions, detail views, contact requests, bookmarks. New Leads ingested from IS24 and merged into the Direkta lead pipeline (deduplicated by email). Alerting if the portal flow breaks (e.g. layout change, Captcha). 5. End-to-End User Flows 5.1 Seller flow — happy path The seller flow is the spine of the product. Every screen in the MVP exists to advance the seller through these eight stages. # Stage What happens 1 Land Seller arrives via SEO, paid ad, or referral. Single CTA: &quot;Find out what your property is worth.&quot; 2 Estimate Seller enters address + 5 fields. Sees an instant indicative price band. No account needed yet. 3 Account To get the full report and proceed, seller creates an account (email + password or magic link). 4 Onboard Seller completes the property record (M1 inputs), uploads photos, and uploads or enters Energieausweis data. 5 Review &amp; Publish Seller reviews AI-generated listing (text + price), can edit any field, then publishes. Compliance check runs at this gate. 6 Distribute Listing goes live on the public landing page; optional one-click syndication to portals (with seller's portal credentials or via Direkta as authorised lister). 7 Operate Leads come in, qualified automatically. Seller responds to ranked leads, schedules viewings, receives offers. 8 Close Seller picks an offer in the dashboard, accepts, downloads a Reservierungsvereinbarung, hands the case to a notary, marks the deal as closed. 5.2 Buyer flow — happy path # Stage What happens 1 Discover Buyer finds the listing on a portal or via direct link to the landing page. 2 View Buyer browses photos, reads the description, downloads the Energieausweis as PDF. 3 Enquire Buyer fills out the enquiry form. No account required. 4 Qualify Within 5 minutes, buyer receives an automated email with three structured questions (budget, financing, timing) and a one-click reply form. 5 Schedule If qualified, buyer is offered a set of seller-defined viewing slots and books one. Buyer receives a calendar invite. 6 View in person Buyer attends viewing. After viewing, buyer receives a one-click &quot;Submit offer&quot; form. 7 Offer Buyer enters offer amount, uploads financing proof and ID, submits. 8 Outcome Buyer receives accept / decline / counter-offer notification. If accepted, buyer is invited into the notary workflow. 5.3 Edge flows that must work Beyond the happy path, the MVP must handle the following edge flows. Engineering is required to design state machines that explicitly cover these transitions. Seller pauses a listing (&quot;on hold&quot;) and resumes it later. Leads must be retained, viewings rescheduled. Seller withdraws a listing entirely. Buyers in active conversations must be notified. Buyer withdraws an offer before acceptance. Seller declines an accepted offer's buyer (rare but legal until notarisation). Two offers come in for the same amount within minutes. Tie-break by financing strength. Buyer requests a viewing outside seller-defined slots. Goes into a manual approval queue. Buyer's enquiry email contains GDPR-relevant data the buyer wants deleted later. Exposé Agent dialog interrupted (browser close, network drop) — must resume from last confirmed state, not restart. IS24 portal flow fails mid-syndication (Captcha, layout change) — listing remains live on Direkta, retry queued, seller notified within 1 hour. Lead arrives via IS24 for the same email already in Direkta lead pipeline — must deduplicate, not double-create. 5.4 Exposé Agent dialog flow The Exposé Agent is the primary onboarding path for non-power-user sellers. It runs as a conversational interface and progresses through five logical phases. The agent decides when to advance based on what is known and what is missing — phases are not rigid screens. # Phase What the agent does 1 Greet &amp; frame Greets the seller in German, explains the process in two sentences, asks for the property type and rough location. 2 Collect basics Asks targeted, one-at-a-time questions for type, address, area, rooms, year built, condition. Validates each as it goes. 3 Collect documents Requests photo upload (with guidance on which rooms to capture). Requests Energieausweis upload or asks for the values manually. 4 Draft &amp; self-review Generates draft long description and short summary. Runs the quality rubric. Re-drafts if any criterion fails. Triggers pricing engine and surfaces the price band with rationale. 5 Confirm &amp; hand off Presents the complete Exposé to the seller for review. Highlights any assumptions made. Hands off to the standard publish flow (M1 step 5). 5.5 Listing Agent (IS24) flow # Stage What happens 1 Connect Seller adds IS24 credentials in account settings. Direkta verifies login in a sandboxed run. Credentials are encrypted at rest. 2 Schedule When the Direkta listing is published, a SyndicationJob is enqueued for IS24. 3 Push Worker logs in, fills the IS24 listing form using property + media data, publishes, and stores the resulting IS24 listing ID. 4 Sync (daily) Worker logs in, scrapes the seller's IS24 dashboard for the listing's stats and inbound contacts, persists deltas as PortalStat and Lead records. 5 Update When the seller edits the listing in Direkta, an update job is enqueued and the IS24 listing is patched. 6 Withdraw When the listing is paused or closed in Direkta, the IS24 listing is paused or removed accordingly. 6. Functional Requirements (Lastenheft) This section specifies what the system must do. Each requirement is identified, prioritised (M = MUST, S = SHOULD, C = COULD for MVP), and given an acceptance criterion. RFC 2119 keywords apply. 6.1 Authentication &amp; Account ID Pri Requirement Acceptance Criterion F-AUTH-01 M The system MUST allow account creation via email + password or email magic link. User can sign up and log in within 60 seconds with no third-party login required. F-AUTH-02 M The system MUST send a verification email before account features become available. Unverified users cannot publish a listing. F-AUTH-03 M The system MUST allow password reset via email. Reset link is valid for 30 minutes and single-use. F-AUTH-04 S The system SHOULD support social login via Google and Apple. Sign-in with Apple and Google work end-to-end. F-AUTH-05 M Sessions MUST expire after 30 days of inactivity. Inactive session is invalidated server-side. F-AUTH-06 M The system MUST allow account deletion with full data erasure within 30 days (GDPR Art. 17). Deletion request triggers an irreversible job after a 14-day grace period. 6.2 Module M1 — Listing Creation ID Pri Requirement Acceptance Criterion F-M1-01 M The system MUST validate the property address against a German address provider (e.g. Google Places, OpenStreetMap Nominatim). Address must resolve to (lat, lng, postcode, city, street, number) before continuing. F-M1-02 M The system MUST support the property types ETW, EFH, MFH (≤4 units), DHH, RH, Grundstück. Each property type renders its specific input form correctly. F-M1-03 M The system MUST accept Energieausweis data either by structured input or by PDF upload with extracted values surfaced for confirmation. PDF upload extracts at least: type (Verbrauchsausweis / Bedarfsausweis), validity date, energy class, value in kWh/(m²·a). F-M1-04 M The system MUST accept between 6 and 30 photos in JPEG or PNG, max 25 MB each. Upload form rejects files outside this range with a clear error. F-M1-05 M The system MUST automatically enhance photos (perspective, brightness, white balance) without altering room contents. An enhanced photo MUST NOT add, remove, or modify objects, only adjust colour, exposure and geometry. F-M1-06 M The system MUST generate a German-language long description and a short summary in under 30 seconds. Median generation time across 50 test listings is below 30 seconds. F-M1-07 M The seller MUST be able to edit any AI-generated text before publishing. Edits persist; regeneration does not silently overwrite seller edits. F-M1-08 M The system MUST run a compliance check before publish and block publish on any failed check. Missing Energieausweis, missing mandatory GEG fields, or missing minimum photo count blocks publish. F-M1-09 S The system SHOULD display a quality score (0–100) for the listing based on completeness and predicted attractiveness. Score is shown above the publish button and updates live. 6.3 Module M2 — Pricing Engine ID Pri Requirement Acceptance Criterion F-M2-01 M The system MUST produce a price band (low / median / high) for any complete property record. 100% of complete records receive a price band within 10 seconds. F-M2-02 M The system MUST present three strategy presets: Quick Sale, Realistic, Maximum, each with a target price. All three presets render with explicit numeric targets and expected time-to-sale ranges. F-M2-03 M The system MUST display the comparable transactions (anonymised) used in the calculation. Min. 5 comparables, max. 20, with similarity score. F-M2-04 M The system MUST display a confidence indicator (high / medium / low). Confidence is high when ≥ 10 comparables within ≤ 1 km and ≤ 12 months exist; low when fewer than 3. F-M2-05 M The pricing function MUST be deterministic and re-derivable. Re-running the engine on the same inputs produces an identical price band. F-M2-06 M The seller MUST be able to override the recommended price. Override is logged and surfaced in the offer dashboard later. F-M2-07 S The system SHOULD track local market trends per postcode and surface them. Postcode-level €/m² 24-month trend chart on the pricing screen. 6.4 Module M3 — Lead Funnel ID Pri Requirement Acceptance Criterion F-M3-01 M Each published listing MUST have a unique, SEO-friendly landing page URL. URL pattern is /immobilien/{city-slug}/{property-slug}-{shortid}. F-M3-02 M The landing page MUST include all GEG-mandatory fields visible without scrolling past the fold on a 1366×768 viewport. Energieausweis-Pflichtangaben pass the audit by an external compliance reviewer. F-M3-03 M Buyers MUST be able to submit an enquiry without creating an account. Form submission with email and message is accepted; buyer receives a confirmation. F-M3-04 M The system MUST send the buyer an automated qualification email within 5 minutes of enquiry. P95 latency from enquiry to email send is &lt; 5 minutes. F-M3-05 M The system MUST compute a Lead Quality Score (0–100) per qualified enquiry. Score formula is documented in this spec (see 10.3) and reproducible. F-M3-06 M The seller MUST see a ranked list of leads sorted by score. Default sort: score desc; secondary: most recent. F-M3-07 M The seller MUST be able to schedule viewings against pre-defined available slots. Slot bookings produce calendar invites for both seller and buyer (.ics). F-M3-08 S The system SHOULD ingest portal-forwarded enquiry emails and parse them into leads. AI Layer Specification AI is plumbing in Direkta, not the product. Three AI-driven capabilities exist in the MVP. Each is wrapped behind an internal interface so the underlying model can be swapped without touching product code. 10.1 Capability A — Listing text generation Generates the German-language Exposé long description and the short summary from the structured property record. Constraints Output language: German, formal Sie-form, neutral marketing tone. Long description: 250–600 words, structured into three paragraphs (property, surroundings, opportunity). Short summary: max 160 characters, no exclamation marks, includes type, rooms, area and city. MUST NOT invent attributes not present in the structured input. The system MUST reject and regenerate any output that contains unverifiable claims. MUST end with a standard disclaimer about Energieausweis values. Provider contract interface TextGenerationProvider { generateLongDescription(input: PropertyDescriptionInput): Promise&lt;string&gt;; generateShortSummary(input: PropertyDescriptionInput): Promise&lt;string&gt;; } type PropertyDescriptionInput = { type: PropertyType; livingArea: number; rooms: number; bathrooms: number; yearBuilt?: number; condition: PropertyCondition; city: string; postcode: string; attributes: string[]; // ['balcony', 'fireplace', 'garage'] energyClass: string; energyValue: number; highlights: string[]; // seller-provided }; 10.2 Capability B — Buyer qualification Generates outbound qualification emails to buyers and parses inbound replies into a structured (budget, financingState, timing) tuple. Constraints Outbound emails MUST sound human and not start with &quot;Sehr geehrte/r Interessent/in&quot;. Inbound parsing MUST default each field to UNKNOWN if confidence &lt; 0.7. Free-text reply that contains an explicit decline (&quot;kein Interesse&quot;, &quot;abgesagt&quot;) MUST set the lead status to DECLINED automatically. 10.3 Capability C — Offer scoring Produces four sub-scores (amount, financing, timing, risk) and a composite for each offer. Score formulae (deterministic, no LLM) scoreAmount = clamp01( (offerAmount - askingPrice * 0.85) / (askingPrice * 0.20) ) * 100 // 85% asking = 0, 105% asking = 100, capped scoreFinance = { CASH: 100, PRE_APPROVED: 85, IN_PROCESS: 55, NONE: 15, UNKNOWN: 30 }[offer.financingState] scoreTiming = clamp01( 1 - daysBetween(offer.desiredClosingAt, now()) / 180 ) * 100 // closes today =&gt; 100, in 6 months =&gt; 0 scoreRisk = 100 - (idVerified ? 0 : 25) - (financingProofPresent ? 0 : 25) - (offerAmount &lt; askingPrice * 0.80 ? 25 : 0) - (buyerProfileAgeDays &lt; 1 ? 10 : 0) scoreComposite = round( 0.30 * scoreAmount + 0.30 * scoreFinance + 0.20 * scoreTiming + 0.20 * scoreRisk ) LLM is used only to produce the natural-language explanation that accompanies the numeric scores in the UI. The numeric values themselves are deterministic and re-derivable from the offer record. This is a deliberate choice: the seller can audit any score. 10.4 Lead Quality Score leadQualityScore = 25 * (financingState in [CASH, PRE_APPROVED] ? 1 : 0) + 25 * (financingState == IN_PROCESS ? 0.6 : 0) + 25 * (timing in [IMMEDIATELY, WITHIN_3M] ? 1 : 0) + 25 * (timing == WITHIN_6M ? 0.6 : 0) + 15 * (budget != null &amp;&amp; budget &gt;= askingPrice * 0.85 ? 1 : 0) + 10 * (phone != null ? 1 : 0) - 20 * (replyContainsRedFlags(message) ? 1 : 0) // clamp to 0..100 10.5 Exposé Agent specification The Exposé Agent is the only LLM-driven component in the system that operates as a multi-turn agent rather than a single prompt. This section defines its loop, its tool surface, and the guarantees engineering must enforce around it. Agent loop while (run.status == RUNNING &amp;&amp; run.turns &lt; MAX_TURNS) { state = loadConversationState(conversation) prompt = buildPrompt({ systemRules: EXPOSE_AGENT_SYSTEM_PROMPT, workingMemory: state.knownFields, missingFields: state.missingFields, history: state.recentTurns(20), // sliding window availableTools: TOOL_REGISTRY }) decision = llm.complete(prompt) // returns either USER_TURN or TOOL_CALL switch (decision.type) { case TOOL_CALL: result = executeTool(decision.tool, decision.input) persistAgentStep(run, decision.tool, decision.input, result) continue // tool result feeds next loop iteration case USER_TURN: streamToUser(decision.message) persistConversationTurn(role=AGENT, decision.message) break // wait for user reply case COMPLETE: if (qualityRubric.passes(state)) { handoffToListing(state) run.status = SUCCEEDED } else { // re-draft once, then escalate to user } } } Tool surface (Exposé Agent) Tool Purpose address.validate Resolve and geocode a property address. Returns lat/lng/postcode/city or a validation error. energy.extract Extract structured fields from an Energieausweis PDF upload reference. pricing.recommend Run the pricing engine over the current property record. Returns price band + comparables. photo.analyse Optional: classify uploaded photos by room type and detect quality issues. listing.draft Generate the long description and short summary from the current working memory. listing.review Run the quality rubric against a draft. Returns pass/fail per criterion. handoff.commit Finalise: persist Property, Listing (REVIEW status), close conversation. Hard constraints MAX_TURNS = 60. Beyond this, the run is flagged for human review. MAX_COST_CENTS_PER_RUN = 200 (≈ €2). Exceeded runs are aborted with a graceful seller message. The agent MUST NOT call any tool not in TOOL_REGISTRY. Out-of-band tool calls are ignored and logged. The agent MUST NOT execute SQL, write files, or call external URLs directly. All side effects go through the registered tool layer. Every TOOL_CALL and USER_TURN MUST be persisted before the next loop iteration. Crash recovery resumes from the last persisted step. 10.6 Listing Agent specification The Listing Agent is a non-conversational, headful-browser agent that operates against IS24 (and, post-MVP, additional portals) on the seller's behalf. It is goal-driven (&quot;publish this listing on IS24&quot;, &quot;sync stats for this listing&quot;) and is implemented behind the PortalDriver interface. Driver interface interface PortalDriver { readonly portal: Portal; publish(input: PublishInput, ctx: AuthCtx): Promise&lt;PublishResult&gt;; update(input: UpdateInput, ctx: AuthCtx): Promise&lt;void&gt;; pause(externalId: string, ctx: AuthCtx): Promise&lt;void&gt;; withdraw(externalId: string, ctx: AuthCtx): Promise&lt;void&gt;; fetchStats(externalId: string, ctx: AuthCtx): Promise&lt;PortalStatSnapshot&gt;; fetchLeads(externalId: string, since: Date, ctx: AuthCtx): Promise&lt;PortalLead[]&gt;; } class IS24BrowserDriver implements PortalDriver { /* Puppeteer/Playwright */ } class IS24ApiDriver implements PortalDriver { /* future, API-based */ } class OpenImmoFeedDriver implements PortalDriver { /* future, XML feed */ } Operational guarantees Each driver call is wrapped in an AgentRun for full audit traceability. Each portal action is rate-limited per credential (configurable; default 1 action / 8 s). A circuit breaker per portal trips on &gt; 25% failure rate over 30 minutes; breakers can be tripped manually for compliance reasons. All browser sessions are isolated per credential (separate browser contexts, no shared cookies). On Captcha or MFA, the job halts; the seller is notified and given a one-click re-auth flow that hands the seller a controlled browser session to complete the challenge. 11. Engineering, product and founders must read and acknowledge it before M6 enters production. Legal posture ImmobilienScout24 General Terms of Use prohibit automated access to the portal. Operating browser automation against IS24 with seller credentials is in breach of those terms even though the credentials are the seller's own. Precedents under § 4 UWG (unfair competition) exist where IS24 has obtained injunctions against tools that automate listing operations on behalf of users. Direkta must accept this as a real, not theoretical, exposure. If a seller's IS24 account is suspended due to detected automation, Direkta is responsible for restoring the seller's listing on alternative channels and absorbing the disruption. Operational risks Bot-detection: IS24 deploys active anti-automation (Cloudflare, behavioural fingerprinting). Detection rates vary; expect periodic full breakage requiring driver updates within 24 hours. UI change: any IS24 layout change can break the driver. SLOs assume up to 1 hour of outage per change, with auto-rollback to manual mode. Captcha and MFA: cannot be solved automatically by Direkta. The system MUST hand control back to the seller to solve any challenge. Mitigations baked into the architecture PortalDriver abstraction: the IS24BrowserDriver is replaceable by IS24ApiDriver or OpenImmoFeedDriver without product code changes. Per-credential isolation: separate browser contexts; no shared session state across sellers. Conservative pacing: default 1 action per 8 seconds, jittered. Explicit, time-stamped seller consent. The seller must understand they are authorising Direkta to act on their behalf, and that automated access may breach IS24 terms. Circuit breaker: ≥ 25% failure rate over 30 minutes pauses all syndication for the affected portal across all sellers, until a human resumes. Migration plan: by month 6 of operation, Direkta must be on either an IS24 contractual relationship (API or OpenImmo) or migrating off IS24 as a primary distribution channel. Founder-level decisions required before launch Sign-off from legal counsel that the consent-gated, seller-credential model is the position Direkta is willing to defend. Decision on whether to disclose the automation approach in seller-facing copy (recommended: yes). Budget and timeline for IS24 commercial conversation in parallel to MVP launch. 12. Compliance, Legal &amp; Trust Direkta operates in a regulated domain. Engineering must understand which legal regimes affect the product surface. The product owner remains responsible for legal correctness; engineering is responsible for not making compliance impossible by design. 13.1 GDPR (DSGVO) Lawful basis: contract (sellers), legitimate interest (buyer enquiry initial contact), consent (marketing emails). Data subject rights: access (Art. 15), rectification (Art. 16), erasure (Art. 17), portability (Art. 20). Each MUST be exercisable from the account UI within 30 days. Data Processing Agreements MUST be signed with every sub-processor (LLM, email, storage, analytics). A public Verzeichnis von Verarbeitungstätigkeiten template MUST be maintained from day one. 13.2 GEG / Energieausweis Every public listing MUST display: Energieausweis type (Verbrauch / Bedarf), validity, energy class, energy value, primary energy source, year of construction. Listings without a valid Energieausweis MUST NOT publish (with narrow exceptions for protected buildings; out of scope for MVP). 13.3 GwG (anti-money-laundering) If Direkta acts as a Makler in the legal sense, it triggers GwG identification duties. The MVP positions Direkta as a software platform, not as a Makler, but a legal opinion MUST be obtained before launch. The product nonetheless verifies buyer identity at offer time as a trust feature. 13.4 Telemediengesetz / TTDSG Cookie banner with explicit consent, granular per category, GDPR-grade. Imprint (Impressum) and Datenschutzerklärung accessible from every page footer. 13.5 Liability and disclaimers All AI-generated text MUST be reviewable by the seller before publication. The seller, not Direkta, is the legal author of the listing. Price recommendations MUST be presented as recommendations, never as guarantees. Direkta does not give legal advice; the notary handoff explicitly hands legal responsibility to the notary. 13.6 Trust architecture (product-level) Trust is a product feature, not a marketing claim. The following capabilities MUST be present: Transparent comparables: every price recommendation shows the underlying comparables. Audit log visible to the seller: every action on their listing in chronological order. Human escalation: a visible &quot;Talk to a human&quot; link, two clicks max, on every authenticated screen. No dark patterns: no urgency timers, no fake activity indicators, no buyer count inflation. Reversible actions: every state transition has an undo within 24 hours, except notary handoff. 14.
